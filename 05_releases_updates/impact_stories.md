# Impact Stories

This page summarizes public impact cases that illustrate how RegulonDB accelerates research, enables reproducible analysis, and supports the broader life sciences community.

## Scope

- Public-facing evidence of scientific and technical impact.
- Narrative format supported by citable publications or traceable outputs.
- Complementary to citation lists and release documentation.

## Story 1: Accelerating Experimental Discovery

### Context
High-throughput studies in *E. coli* require trusted reference knowledge to validate newly detected regulatory elements.

### RegulonDB Contribution
RegulonDB provides curated regulatory objects and evidence-linked annotations that enable rapid contextualization of new findings.

### Public Impact

- Faster interpretation of new promoter and regulatory observations.
- Improved comparability between newly generated datasets and established knowledge.
- Reduced duplication of manual evidence collection.

### Evidence

- Jago et al. (2025), *Nature Communications* (PMID: 39824824).

## Story 2: Enabling Computational Method Development

### Context
Network inference and systems biology workflows benefit from high-confidence prior knowledge.

### RegulonDB Contribution
RegulonDB offers expert-curated regulatory interactions and evidence frameworks that can be integrated into computational pipelines.

### Public Impact

- Better-informed model constraints in gene regulatory network inference.
- More robust benchmarking and method validation.
- Reuse of curated prior knowledge in microbial data-science workflows.

### Evidence

- Stock et al. (2025), *Molecular Systems Biology* (PMID: 39939367).
- Guharajan et al. (2025), *Nucleic Acids Research* (PMID: 39921566).

## Story 3: Supporting Comparative and Cross-Species Analysis

### Context
Comparative genomics requires transferable regulatory logic from well-annotated organisms to less-characterized systems.

### RegulonDB Contribution
RegulonDB provides a structured, evidence-based regulatory model that supports transfer and validation across microbial resources.

### Public Impact

- Reuse in comparative frameworks for bacterial regulation.
- Improved interpretation of conserved regulatory associations.
- Expanded utility beyond a single-organism reference context.

### Evidence

- Buzzao et al. (2025), FunCoup 6, *Nucleic Acids Research* (PMID: 39530220).

## Story 4: Improving Research Efficiency Through Reuse

### Context
Regulatory evidence is often distributed across many publications and formats.

### RegulonDB Contribution
RegulonDB integrates curated evidence, standardized objects, and persistent references in a reusable public resource.

### Public Impact

- Lower integration cost for external analyses.
- Faster access to curated regulatory knowledge.
- Better reproducibility through traceable evidence and references.

## Story 5: Supporting Education and Skills Development

### Context
Training in regulatory genomics requires realistic datasets and interpretable biological objects.

### RegulonDB Contribution
RegulonDB provides structured, curation-backed data that can be used in teaching and practical training.

### Public Impact

- Improved learning in motif analysis and regulatory interpretation.
- Training support for students and early-career researchers.
- Broader adoption of reproducible data practices.

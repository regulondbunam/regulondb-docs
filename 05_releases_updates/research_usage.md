# Research Usage

This page summarizes how RegulonDB is used in scientific research, based on public evidence and reproducible indicators suitable for portal publication.

## Purpose

To provide a transparent and updateable public view of research uptake, citation relevance, and methodological reuse of RegulonDB.

## Public Indicator Scope

This report focuses on indicators that can be shared publicly:

- Citation of RegulonDB core publications.
- Reuse in computational and experimental studies.
- Presence in benchmarking, validation, and comparative workflows.

Operational or institutionally sensitive analytics are reported only in aggregated form when appropriate.

## Evidence Categories

## 1. Citation-Based Evidence

- Core RegulonDB publications are cited in peer-reviewed literature across microbiology, systems biology, and bioinformatics.
- Versioned publications provide traceable reference points for reproducible citation.

See: [How to Cite RegulonDB](../00_about_policies/how_to_cite.md)

## 2. Methodological Reuse Evidence

RegulonDB is reused in:

- Gene regulatory network inference workflows.
- Validation of high-throughput regulatory datasets.
- Comparative analyses and cross-resource integration tasks.

## 3. Community Adoption Signals

- Use in educational and training settings.
- Use in computational benchmark development.
- Reuse in studies requiring curated evidence with provenance.

## Public Summary Table (Template)

| Indicator | Public reporting format | Data level |
|---|---|---|
| Citation uptake | Yearly aggregate trend summary | Aggregated |
| Reuse in methods papers | Curated list of representative studies | Public references |

## Reporting Method

- Primary sources: peer-reviewed publications and public documentation references.
- Update frequency: at least annually or when major evidence accumulates.
- Governance: reviewed by documentation and governance team before publication updates.

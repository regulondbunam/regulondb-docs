# Global Dimension

RegulonDB is developed and maintained at the Center for Genomic Sciences, National Autonomous University of Mexico (UNAM), and serves a global scientific community.

Although operations are primarily based in Mexico, the resource has sustained international relevance through global data reuse, cross-resource interoperability, and diverse international scientific collaborations, some of them lasting decades. This has contributed to constant improvements, shared curation practices, all of this reflected in shared peer-reviewed publications.

## International Scientific Role

RegulonDB functions as a reference knowledge base for transcriptional regulation in *Escherichia coli* K-12 and supports researchers across multiple domains ofknowledge and disciplines, including microbiology, systems biology, bioinformatics, and computational biology, with a strong reference as the best mechanistically known gene regulation network in biology.

Its impact extends beyond local use through:

- Broad international citation in scientific literature.
- Reuse of curated knowledge in external analytical workflows and tools.
- Continuous use in education, training, and methods benchmarking.
- Major regulatory network reference used for instance: i) in the assessment for experimental novel methods, ii) for modeling with diverse formal (mathematical, computational, logic..) and predictive methods and pipelines, including inference and artificial intelligence methods, iii) as the place of emergence for new concepts like network motifs or logical gates, iv)for conceptual and ontology novel frameworks. 

## International Collaboration and Interoperability

RegulonDB is integrated into the global biodata ecosystem through links and interoperability with external resources and standards.

Examples include:

- Cross-references to external identifiers and literature databases.
- Use of controlled vocabularies and ontology-based annotation.
- Alignment with FAIR-oriented documentation and access practices.

These connections support data discoverability, reuse, and integration across platforms.

## Funding and Institutional Context

RegulonDB is institutionally supported by UNAM and has received support from national and international funding sources, including agencies from Mexico and the United States.

This mixed support model has enabled long-term continuity of:

- Expert biocuration.
- Software and infrastructure development.
- Data dissemination and technical support.

For funding details, see: [Funding Sources](funding_credits.md).

## Open Access and Community Reach

RegulonDB is openly accessible through its web platform and associated documentation.

The project promotes international use by providing:

- Public documentation for data interpretation and reuse.
- Programmatic access and downloadable resources.
- Contact and support channels for users and collaborators.

This approach enables contribution to global research workflows while maintaining transparent curation and governance practices.

## Governance and Continuous Improvement

The resource evolves through coordinated work among curation, software, and infrastructure teams, with scientific guidance and periodic updates to policies and documentation.

Global relevance is maintained by:

- Updating content in response to scientific and technical advances.
- Strengthening interoperability and documentation quality.
- Preserving traceability between curated objects, evidence, and literature.

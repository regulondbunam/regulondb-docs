# Governance and Scientific Advisory

This page describes the public governance framework of RegulonDB and the role of its Scientific Advisory Board (SAB).

## Purpose

To provide transparent information on how strategic, scientific, and operational decisions are guided and reviewed for the long-term sustainability of RegulonDB.

## Governance Scope

The governance model covers:

- Scientific priorities and curation quality direction.
- Technical and infrastructure continuity planning.
- Documentation and policy maintenance.
- Community responsiveness and strategic alignment.

## Governance Structure

RegulonDB governance is coordinated through three functional layers:

1. Project leadership and coordination.
2. Biocuration operations.
3. Software and infrastructure operations.

These layers operate with periodic strategic input from the Scientific Advisory Board.

## Scientific Advisory Board (SAB)

The SAB provides external expert guidance on scientific quality, strategic direction, and international alignment.

### Current Members

- [Dr. Steve Busby](https://research.birmingham.ac.uk/en/persons/steve-busby) (University of Birmingham, UK)
- [Dr. Jacques van Helden](https://tagc.univ-amu.fr/fr/annuaire/van-helden-jacques) (Aix-Marseille Universite, France)
- [Dr. James Galagan](https://www.galaganlab.org/) (Boston University, USA)
- [Dr. Daniela Ledezma Tejeida](https://www.ccg.unam.mx/daniela-elizabeth-ledezma-tejeida/) (Center for Genomic Sciences, Mexico)
- [Dr. Ernesto Perez Rueda](https://pcyt.unam.mx/index.php/erueda/) (UNAM, Mexico)

### Core Functions

- Advise on scientific and technical priorities.
- Evaluate strategic opportunities and long-term positioning.
- Support quality assurance and relevance to community needs.
- Provide domain-specific consultation on emerging topics.

### Working Modality

- The SAB meets at least twice per year in structured review sessions.
- Additional ad hoc consultations may be held when needed.
- Recommendations are reviewed by the RegulonDB leadership and operational teams for implementation planning.

## Roles and Responsibilities (Public Summary)

- Project leadership: strategic coordination, institutional articulation, and final decision integration.
- Biocuration team: scientific data quality, evidence traceability, and curation consistency.
- Software and infrastructure team: platform continuity, technical implementation, and service operation.
- SAB: external scientific guidance and strategic recommendations.

## Decision and Review Cycle

- Annual planning cycle for priorities, deliverables, and policy updates.
- Periodic review of progress, risks, and improvement opportunities.
- Documentation updates published in the portal when governance-relevant changes occur.

## Conflict of Interest (COI)

RegulonDB seeks objective, science-driven advisory input. When relevant, potential conflicts of interest in advisory participation are expected to be declared and managed by the project leadership according to institutional good-practice standards.
